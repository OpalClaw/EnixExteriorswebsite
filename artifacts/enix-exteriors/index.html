When It's Time for a New Roof
ENIX EXTERIORS — RESIDENTIAL ROOFING
When It's Time
for a New Roof
A complete guide for Tennessee homeowners
Know the warning signs before small damage becomes catastrophic
ROOF AGE — THE FIRST INDICATOR
Your Roof's Age Tells the Story
20–25 years: inspect carefully every season
25–30 years: start budgeting for replacement now
30+ years: replacement is overdue — act before the next storm
VISIBLE WARNING SIGNS
What to See from the Ground
Curling, cupping, or cracking shingle tabs
Granule buildup in gutters after any rain event
Missing shingles, dark patches, or bare roof deck visible
HIDDEN DAMAGE — CHECK YOUR ATTIC
The Damage You Can't See from Outside
Water stains or mold on rafters and sheathing
Daylight visible through cracks in the roof deck
Musty smell or frost on underside of deck in winter
THE COST OF WAITING
Small Problems Become Expensive Fast
A $300 flashing repair → $5,000 rot and framing repair
Water damage spreads with every single rain event
Mold develops within 24–48 hours of sustained saturation
REPAIR OR REPLACE? — THE 50% RULE
Making the Smart Financial Decision
Less than 25% damaged and roof under 15 years old → Repair
More than 25% damaged or roof is 20+ years → Replace
If repair cost exceeds 50% of replacement → Always replace

Commercial Roofing Systems
ENIX EXTERIORS — COMMERCIAL ROOFING
Commercial Roofing
Systems Explained
A guide for Tennessee business and property owners
Understand your options before committing to any system
WHAT MAKES COMMERCIAL ROOFING DIFFERENT
Flat & Low-Slope Roofs Require a Different Approach
Pitch below 2:12 — water cannot shed by gravity alone
Requires sealed, fully waterproof membranes throughout
Materials, installation, and warranty are entirely different from shingles
TPO MEMBRANE ROOFING
TPO: America's Most Installed Commercial Membrane
White surface reflects up to 90% of UV — cuts HVAC costs 15–25%
Heat-welded seams stronger than the membrane itself
15–25 year lifespan with quality 60-mil installation
Best for energy-conscious buildings and simple flat roofs
MODIFIED BITUMEN ROOFING
Modified Bitumen: 40+ Years of Proven Performance
Multi-layer asphalt system with redundant waterproofing
Outstanding resistance to hail, foot traffic, and puncture
Applied by torch, cold adhesive, or self-adhering membrane
Ideal for buildings with frequent HVAC crew roof access
EPDM RUBBER ROOFING
EPDM: The Longest Commercial Track Record
Rubber membrane with documented 40–60 year field performance
Outstanding UV and ozone resistance — no chalking or yellowing
Fewer seams than other systems = fewer potential leak points
Lowest material cost among all major commercial systems
WHICH SYSTEM IS RIGHT FOR YOUR BUILDING?
Match Your System to Your Building's Needs
Energy efficiency priority → TPO is the clear choice
High foot traffic + maximum durability → Modified Bitumen
Best proven longevity + lowest material cost → EPDM
Existing sound roof? → Silicone coating can add 10–20 years

After Storm Damage: Action Plan
ENIX EXTERIORS — STORM DAMAGE GUIDE
After Storm Damage:
Your Action Plan
What to do in the critical hours after a severe storm
The right steps protect both your home and your insurance claim
STEP 1 — SAFETY FIRST
Stay Safe: Don't Go on the Roof
Check for downed power lines before stepping outside
Assess structural damage from the ground — stay off the roof
If walls or ceilings are sagging or buckling — evacuate immediately
STEP 2 — DOCUMENT EVERYTHING FIRST
Photos Before Any Cleanup or Tarping
Photograph and video all damage from every angle — ground level
Document interior water stains, wet ceilings, and damaged belongings
Screenshot NWS storm alerts and reports confirming the event
STEP 3 — CALL A ROOFER BEFORE YOUR INSURER
Professional Documentation Strengthens Your Claim
A contractor provides professional written damage assessment
On-roof inspection finds damage invisible from the ground
Your contractor meets the adjuster at inspection — ensuring nothing is missed
STEP 4 — EMERGENCY TARPING IF NEEDED
Stop Further Damage Within 24–48 Hours
Active leaks need tarping immediately — water damage compounds fast
Professional tarping is covered by your insurance claim
Never sign an Assignment of Benefits (AOB) for tarping or repairs
STEP 5 — FILE YOUR CLAIM WITH FULL DOCUMENTATION
File Promptly — Don't Wait
Most Tennessee policies allow 1 year from the storm date — act now
Include your contractor assessment, all photos, and storm records
Request your contractor be present at the adjuster's on-site inspection
